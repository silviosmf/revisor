This research investigates the most effective language style (abstract or concrete) for OTAs chatbots to apologize to the customer to gain forgiveness for service failure. Results of three experimental studies with 348, 452, and 470 sample sizes showed that the overall concrete language style of the OTAs chatbot apology was more effective in achieving customer forgiveness. This study explored the variables affecting chatbot technology adoption within MGAs by selecting their top management personnel as respondents. The study identified factors influencing the successful integration of chatbots using the TOE framework. Empirical data were gathered using purposive and snowball sampling, then distributed via email to MGAs. A strong response rate was obtained from 348 targeted respondents, with 262 actively participating. The analysis was conducted using SmartPLS. The hypotheses on the relationships between big data analytics (BDA), organisational learning capability (OLC), government support (GS), organisational readiness (OR), and citizen demand (CD) were validated, verifying their importance in boosting the adoption motivation of chatbots. This study examines how disclosing an AI agent’s identity influences consumers’ information distortion in online service recovery. Building on mind perception theory and psychological distance theory, we propose that AI agent identity disclosure reduces consumers’ mind perception of service agents, which subsequently shapes cognitive trust and increases their tendency to distort information during service recovery interactions. Furthermore, the level of anthropomorphism moderates the relationship.
